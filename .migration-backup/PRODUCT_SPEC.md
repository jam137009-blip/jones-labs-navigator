# Frozen Hackathon Product Specification

## Product

Navigator adapts depth to demonstrated domain fluency, never confidence of tone alone. The inferred exploratory, informed, or expert mode is visible and correctable. Expert users remain the primary domain authority; Navigator improves the completeness and challenge-resistance of the understanding supporting their judgment.

**Jones Labs Navigator** — Apps for Your Life

**Core thesis:** people often possess an intended outcome before they possess the words, prompts, questions, or knowledge needed to express it.

**Core principle:** construct sufficient understanding before making a recommendation.

**Foundational law:** every question must earn its existence by materially reducing relevant uncertainty toward the user’s intended outcome.

**Optimization objective:** maximize relevant understanding per unit of user effort.

## Frozen MVP flow

1. Welcome and privacy framing
2. One free-text intention sample
3. Three generated intention hypotheses; a fourth only when materially distinct
4. Multi-select confirmation plus “None of these” correction
5. At most one adaptive precision question
6. Sufficiency check and bounded final insight
7. One optional refinement
8. Focused local evidence commitment

## Precision-question logic

Navigator must identify the intended outcome, explicit facts, inferences, competing hypotheses, and greatest remaining uncertainty. It asks one question only when the expected improvement in recommendation quality justifies the user effort. The question should distinguish competing interpretations or fill information essential to the outcome. When fixed options would distort meaning, use free text.

## Sufficiency threshold

Sufficient understanding exists when Navigator can responsibly ground the intended outcome, current reality, desired reality, primary constraint, important hypotheses or unknowns, meaningful decision directions, and one feasible move. If sufficiency remains incomplete after one clarification, Navigator returns its best bounded interpretation, calibrates confidence, explains limitations, and invites one refinement. It never creates an endless interview.

## Final insight order

1. The Question Beneath the Question
2. What Navigator Heard
3. Current Reality
4. Intended Outcome
5. Primary Constraint
6. Assumptions Worth Testing
7. Possible Directions
8. Next Move
9. Confidence and what could change the interpretation

Process narration is intentionally omitted from the result; the space belongs to information that can change the user’s decision.

## Council optimization gate

Before any question, option, interpretation, or recommendation, Navigator privately checks mission relevance, direct relevance, information value, uncertainty reduction, sufficiency, evidence grounding, and whether removal would preserve understanding. If an interaction does not earn its cognitive cost, it is omitted.

## Trust requirements

- Never invent facts, diagnose, infer causes as certainty, or promise outcomes.
- Every assumption is visibly labeled as a hypothesis.
- No more than three decision paths and exactly one next move.
- Human authority remains explicit.
- Urgent-risk language pauses ordinary recommendations.
- Professional topics include limitations.
- Raw consultation content is not intentionally logged or permanently stored.

## Exclusions

Accounts, billing, subscriptions, notifications, permanent profiles or history, public sharing, dashboards, voice, languages, native apps, autonomous browsing, vector databases, full multi-agent orchestration, gamification, dark mode, and long-term accountability scoring.
